## Academic Calendar & Semester Structure

Chronos Academy operates on a **Closed-Loop Trimester** system. Because campus locations span multiple historical eras, a proprietary timekeeping framework — the **Absolute Reckoning (AR)** — is used to synchronize all academic schedules regardless of local calendar systems.

### How a Trimester Works

| Phase | Duration (AR) | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Orientation Week** | 1 AR-week | Students receive era-specific inoculations, cultural briefings, and chrono-sickness acclimation. Temporal anchors are calibrated. |
| **Instruction Period** | 10 AR-weeks | Courses are taught at their designated Era/Campus. Students transit between eras via Academy-regulated Slip Gates. |
| **Field Displacement** | 2 AR-weeks | Practicum and immersion assignments. Students are embedded in their study era without modern equipment (except emergency recall beacons). |
| **Convergence Week** | 1 AR-week | All students and faculty converge on the Nexus Campus (2045 CE, Geneva) for cross-era examinations, symposia, and grade reconciliation. |
| **Temporal Rest** | 2 AR-weeks | Mandatory decompression. Students return to their native timeline. Chrono-medical checkups required. |

**Total trimester length:** 16 AR-weeks
**Trimesters per academic year:** 3 (Autumn Loop, Spring Loop, Summer Intensive)

> **Note on Subjective Time:** Due to relativistic effects at certain campuses (particularly the Deep Antiquity and Far Future stations), students may experience subjective time dilation. The Registrar's Office certifies that no student will age more than 1.3 standard years per academic year, per Academy charter.

## Degree Programs

### 1. Bachelor of Temporal Engineering (B.T.E.)

**Duration:** 4 academic years (12 trimesters)
**Core Focus:** Design, maintenance, and theory of time-displacement technology. Graduates are certified to operate and repair Slip Gates, stabilize temporal anomalies, and construct chrono-shielded environments.
**Required Departments:** Temporal Engineering (8 courses), Paradox Sciences (4 courses), electives (4 courses).

### 2. Bachelor of Cross-Era Diplomacy (B.C.D.)

**Duration:** 4 academic years
**Core Focus:** Negotiation, governance, and peacekeeping across historical periods. Graduates serve as temporal ambassadors, mediating between era-locked civilizations and preventing anachronistic interference.
**Required Departments:** Chrono-Diplomacy & Governance (7 courses), Philosophy & Ethics (4 courses), Historical Immersion & Survival (3 courses), electives (2 courses).

### 3. Master of Paradox Sciences (M.P.S.)

**Duration:** 2 academic years (6 trimesters), requires an undergraduate degree
**Core Focus:** Advanced theoretical chronophysics, grandfather paradox resolution, and multiverse branching analysis. Graduates work in the Academy's Paradox Containment Division or in independent temporal research.
**Required Departments:** Paradox Sciences (6 courses), Temporal Engineering (2 courses), thesis project.

### 4. Bachelor of Chrono-Cultural Studies (B.C.S.)

**Duration:** 4 academic years
**Core Focus:** Art, language, literature, and cultural practice across all human eras. Graduates become curators, translators, cultural preservationists, or era-specific creative practitioners.
**Required Departments:** Arts, Language & Cultural Continuity (7 courses), Historical Immersion & Survival (4 courses), Philosophy & Ethics (3 courses), electives (2 courses).

### Department of Temporal Engineering (TEMP)

---

#### TEMP 101 — Foundations of Slip Gate Mechanics

- **Department:** Temporal Engineering
- **Level:** Introductory
- **Era/Campus:** Industrial Revolution Campus (1890s London)
- **Instructor:** Nikola Tesla
- **Description:** An introduction to the physics and engineering principles behind Slip Gate technology. Students learn the fundamentals of temporal displacement fields, energy requirements for era-transit, and the basic safety architecture that prevents catastrophic timeline rupture. Laboratory sessions take place in Tesla's personally designed Chrono-Workshop beneath the London campus.
- **Prerequisites:** None
- **Student Warning:** Professor Tesla insists on live demonstrations involving extremely high voltages. Rubber-soled boots are mandatory. Do not touch the Tesla Coils, no matter how much he encourages you.

---

#### TEMP 205 — Chrono-Navigation & Anchor Calibration

- **Department:** Temporal Engineering
- **Level:** Intermediate
- **Era/Campus:** Age of Exploration Campus (early 1800s, American West)
- **Instructor:** Benjamin Banneker
- **Description:** Students master the art and science of temporal navigation — pinpointing exact arrival coordinates across both space and time. The course covers anchor calibration (ensuring you arrive where *and* when you intend), drift compensation for long-range jumps, and emergency re-routing protocols. Practical exercises involve navigating to increasingly precise historical moments.
- **Prerequisites:** TEMP 101
- **Student Warning:** Miscalibration during practicals has, on rare occasion, deposited students in the wrong century. Always verify your anchor lock before displacement. The buddy system is not optional.

---

#### TEMP 310 — Temporal Shielding & Containment Architecture

- **Department:** Temporal Engineering
- **Level:** Intermediate
- **Era/Campus:** Nexus Campus (2045 CE, Geneva)
- **Instructor:** Marie Curie
- **Description:** This course examines the engineering of chrono-shielded environments — spaces that are sealed against temporal interference, paradox bleed, and unauthorized era-transit. Students design and build miniature containment chambers, study the radiation profiles of unstable time fields, and learn to identify structural weaknesses in existing shields. Professor Curie brings unmatched expertise in understanding invisible, dangerous forces.
- **Prerequisites:** TEMP 101, PARA 150
- **Student Warning:** Professor Curie's lab safety standards are rigorous and non-negotiable. She has seen what happens when invisible forces are treated carelessly. Respect the dosimeters.

---

#### TEMP 450 — Advanced Slip Gate Design & Construction

- **Department:** Temporal Engineering
- **Level:** Advanced
- **Era/Campus:** Industrial Revolution Campus (1890s London)
- **Instructor:** Nikola Tesla
- **Description:** The capstone engineering course. Students work in teams to design, prototype, and test a functional micro-scale Slip Gate under Tesla's direct supervision. Topics include exotic energy sourcing, multi-era gate synchronization, failsafe architecture, and the ethical constraints imposed by the Academy's Charter on Temporal Non-Interference. Final projects are evaluated by a cross-departmental panel.
- **Prerequisites:** TEMP 205, TEMP 310, PARA 320
- **Student Warning:** Your final project will be a working device. If it works too well, the Paradox Containment Division will confiscate it. This has happened. Do not take it personally.

### Department of Cross-Era Philosophy & Ethics (PHIL)

---

#### PHIL 110 — Ethics of Temporal Interference

- **Department:** Cross-Era Philosophy & Ethics
- **Level:** Introductory
- **Era/Campus:** Classical Antiquity Campus (4th c. Alexandria)
- **Instructor:** Hypatia of Alexandria
- **Description:** The foundational ethics course required of all Chronos Academy students. If you can travel to any era, what are you permitted to change? What are you forbidden from changing? And who decides? Professor Hypatia guides students through a rigorous examination of temporal non-interference doctrine, the moral weight of foreknowledge, and the paradoxes of historical complicity. Socratic dialogue is the primary method of instruction.
- **Prerequisites:** None
- **Student Warning:** Professor Hypatia does not accept "but I could save millions" as a sufficient ethical argument. Come prepared to have your assumptions disassembled with warmth and precision.

---

#### PHIL 240 — The Grandfather Paradox: A Moral Inquiry

- **Department:** Cross-Era Philosophy & Ethics
- **Level:** Intermediate
- **Era/Campus:** Classical Antiquity Campus (4th c. Alexandria)
- **Instructor:** Socrates (Guest Lecturer, coordinated by Dept. Chair Hypatia)
- **Description:** What happens when the logical consequence of your action is the impossibility of your existence? This seminar explores the philosophical dimensions of causal paradoxes, personal identity across branching timelines, and whether moral responsibility persists when the "you" who acted may never have existed. Socrates leads the inquiry through relentless questioning. Students should expect to leave each session with more questions than they arrived with.
- **Prerequisites:** PHIL 110
- **Student Warning:** Students are advised not to challenge Socrates to a debate on the first day. He has been doing this for over two millennia. You will not win. You may, however, learn something.

---

#### PHIL 380 — Temporal Justice: Reparation Across Eras

- **Department:** Cross-Era Philosophy & Ethics
- **Level:** Advanced
- **Era/Campus:** Nexus Campus (2045 CE, Geneva)
- **Instructor:** Frederick Douglass
- **Description:** If time travel makes it possible to witness historical injustice firsthand, does it also create an obligation to address it? This advanced seminar grapples with the intersection of temporal capability and moral duty. Topics include the ethics of retroactive reparation, the doctrine of historical sovereignty, and the danger of imposing present-day values on past civilizations. Professor Douglass brings both intellectual rigor and lived moral authority to every session.
- **Prerequisites:** PHIL 110, PHIL 240, DIPL 200
- **Student Warning:** This course will challenge you. Professor Douglass does not permit comfortable detachment. Be prepared to confront history — and yourself — honestly.

### Department of Historical Immersion & Survival (HIST)

---

#### HIST 101 — Surviving Your First Displacement

- **Department:** Historical Immersion & Survival
- **Level:** Introductory
- **Era/Campus:** Age of Exploration Campus (early 1800s, American West)
- **Instructor:** Sacagawea
- **Description:** A mandatory first-year survival course. Students learn to adapt to unfamiliar environments without modern technology: sourcing clean water, navigating by stars and landmarks, reading weather patterns, building shelter, and — most critically — blending in with local populations without arousing suspicion. Professor Sacagawea emphasizes resourcefulness, cultural humility, and the understanding that survival is inseparable from respect for the land and its people.
- **Prerequisites:** None
- **Student Warning:** Your smartphone will not work in 1805. Your GPS will not work in 1805. Your confidence that you "could totally survive without technology" will not survive 1805. Listen to Professor Sacagawea.

---

#### HIST 215 — Plague, Famine & Conflict: Hazard Navigation Across Eras

- **Department:** Historical Immersion & Survival
- **Level:** Intermediate
- **Era/Campus:** Medieval Campus (12th c. Poitiers) and rotating era-assignments
- **Instructor:** Ibn Sina (Avicenna)
- **Description:** History is full of lethal hazards that modern travelers may fatally underestimate. This course covers medical preparedness for pre-antibiotic eras, conflict-zone navigation, famine survival, and the recognition of early disease symptoms in environments where a common cold could be a death sentence — or where you could inadvertently start a pandemic. Professor Ibn Sina's medical knowledge, centuries ahead of his own time, makes him uniquely suited to bridge ancient and modern survival medicine.
- **Prerequisites:** HIST 101
- **Student Warning:** Era-specific inoculations are required before every field session. Students who skip their inoculations will be pulled from the timeline and given a failing grade. There are no exceptions. Smallpox does not grade on a curve.

#### HIST 340 — Deep Antiquity Field Practicum

- **Department:** Historical Immersion & Survival
- **Level:** Advanced
- **Era/Campus:** Deep Antiquity Station (3000 BCE, Mesopotamia)
- **Instructor:** Enheduanna
- **Description:** A full-immersion practicum in one of the oldest cradles of civilization. Students spend the Field Displacement period embedded in Sumerian society under the guidance of Enheduanna — high priestess, poet, and the earliest known named author in human history. Coursework includes cuneiform literacy, temple administration, agricultural cycles, and navigating the complex social hierarchies of early urban civilization. This is not a simulation.
- **Prerequisites:** HIST 101, HIST 215, ARTL 160
- **Student Warning:** You will be expected to contribute to the temple community during your immersion. "I'm from the future" is not an acceptable excuse for failing to help with the barley harvest.

#### HIST 420 — Covert Observation: The Art of Temporal Fieldwork

- **Department:** Historical Immersion & Survival
- **Level:** Advanced
- **Era/Campus:** Rotating (students are assigned a random era)
- **Instructor:** Harriet Tubman
- **Description:** The most demanding course in the department. Students learn to move through hostile and surveilled historical environments without detection, gather intelligence, and extract themselves cleanly — all while adhering to the Non-Interference Charter. Professor Tubman, who conducted thirteen missions through some of the most dangerous territory in American history without losing a single person, teaches stealth, route planning, reading people, and the discipline required to stay calm when everything depends on it.
- **Prerequisites:** HIST 101, HIST 215, DIPL 200
- **Student Warning:** Professor Tubman's rules are simple: follow instructions, stay quiet, and never panic. Students who cannot follow these rules will be removed from the course for their own safety and the safety of the timeline.

- **Department:** Paradox Sciences & Theoretical Chronophysics
- **Level:** Advanced
- **Era/Campus:** Nexus Campus (2045 CE, Geneva)

## Academic Policies

### Enrollment & Registration

- Students may enroll in a maximum of **4 courses per trimester** (5 with department chair approval).
- Cross-era courses require completion of HIST 101 (Surviving Your First Displacement) and medical clearance from the Chronos Academy Health Service.
- All students, regardless of degree program, must complete the **Core Temporal Curriculum** by the end of their second year: PHIL 110, HIST 101, DIPL 200, and PARA 150.

### The Core Temporal Curriculum (Required of All Students)

| Course | Department | Why It's Required |
|---|---|---|
| PHIL 110 — Ethics of Temporal Interference | Philosophy & Ethics | No student may travel without understanding the moral weight of temporal presence. |
| HIST 101 — Surviving Your First Displacement | Historical Immersion | No student may travel without the practical skills to survive arrival. |
| DIPL 200 — The Non-Interference Charter | Chrono-Diplomacy | No student may travel without knowing the law that governs all temporal activity. |
| PARA 150 — Introduction to Chronophysics | Paradox Sciences | No student may travel without understanding the physics of what they are doing. |

### Grading

Chronos Academy uses a standard letter-grade system (A through F) with the following unique provisions:

- **Temporal Incomplete (TI):** Granted when a student's coursework is disrupted by a verified paradox event, involuntary timeline displacement, or Academy-ordered emergency recall. TI grades do not affect GPA and are resolved within one trimester.
- **Non-Interference Violation (NIV):** A failing notation applied when a student demonstrably interferes with historical events in a manner that violates the Charter. NIV carries automatic academic probation and mandatory review by the Temporal Tribunal.

### Academic Integrity

Academic dishonesty at Chronos Academy includes all conventional forms (plagiarism, fabrication, cheating) as well as several unique to temporal study:

- **Retroactive self-plagiarism:** Submitting work to a past version of yourself for early completion.
- **Temporal exam preview:** Using time travel to view examination content before the scheduled exam date.
- **Anachronistic sourcing:** Citing sources from eras later than the assignment's research scope without disclosure.

Violations are adjudicated by the Temporal Tribunal and may result in sanctions up to and including **permanent timeline restriction** (loss of all Slip Gate access).

### Student Health & Safety

- All students must undergo **chrono-medical screening** before each trimester.
- **Temporal Adjustment Disorder (TAD)** — symptoms include disorientation, phantom memories, and mild chronological confusion — is common in first-year students and is treatable at any campus health center.
- The Academy maintains **emergency recall beacons** for all students engaged in field displacement. Activation of a beacon results in immediate extraction to the Nexus Campus, no questions asked.
